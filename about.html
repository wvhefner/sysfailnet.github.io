I break things
to understand them.

Developer and security researcher interested in Linux internals, systems programming, and what happens at the edges of designed behavior.

This site is where I think in public. Posts range from technical deep-dives to short notes on things I found interesting. The bar for publishing is low — if it took me more than five minutes to figure out, it's probably worth writing down.

What I work on

Day-to-day: Linux systems, Python tooling, and security research. I'm especially interested in the intersection of observability and security — what does a running system actually tell you, and what's it hiding?

Most of my open-source work lives on GitHub.

Get in touch

Reach me at hello@sysfail.net or open an issue on any of my repos. I'm generally happy to talk about Linux, security tooling, or whatever is currently on fire.

This site is static HTML hosted on GitHub Pages. No tracking, no analytics, no JavaScript framework. Just text.
