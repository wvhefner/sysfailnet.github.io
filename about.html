About

Long experience, still learning on purpose.

I'm a cybersecurity practitioner with 25 years of experience and a sustained interest in how adversarial behavior evolves across technical systems, narrative environments, and emerging domains. My work is grounded in practice, but I'm most energized by the places where practice meets deeper analysis.

Professional orientation

I'm drawn to security problems that are strategically meaningful, operationally real, and resistant to shallow answers. Cyber operations and cognitive or information warfare sit at that intersection, because both require technical understanding and a clear view of how people, incentives, and systems interact.

What I'm building toward

In addition to my core interests, I'm investing more attention in hardware-oriented learning and cyberbiosecurity. Both feel important not because they are fashionable, but because they open up new classes of security questions that deserve more disciplined treatment.

Interests

Areas that hold my attention

Cyber Operations

Operational security, adversary tradecraft, detection, response, and the real-world mechanics of defending systems under pressure.

Cognitive & Information Warfare

Influence, perception, propaganda, narrative shaping, and how technology changes the reach and economics of informational conflict.

Hardware Learning

Embedded systems, physical attack surfaces, and the security implications of moving closer to the devices and components underneath software.

Cyberbiosecurity

The growing overlap between biological systems and digital infrastructure, including the attack surfaces, data issues, and governance gaps that come with it.

Approach

How I tend to think about security

Operational realism

I care about what survives contact with real systems, real users, and real adversaries.

System-level thinking

I'm interested in how technical, social, and institutional layers reinforce or undermine one another.

Deliberate curiosity

Even after a long career, the useful posture is still to keep learning into the places where the field is incomplete.

Cybersecurity practice with long operational memory and active research interests in cyber operations, information conflict, hardware learning, and cyberbiosecurity.
